Films in Manhattan

"I look out the window and I see the lights and the skyline and the people on the street rushing around looking for action, love, and the world's greatest chocolate chip cookie, and my heart does a little dance."
-- Nora Ephron

An Affair to Remember - 1957
Starring Carey Grant and Deborah Kerr, This classic Hollywood movie features a very important scene at the top of the Empire State Building. We can't say more without spoiling the ending -- just watch it!

King Kong - 1933
Any list of New York films has to include one of the most famous images of the city ever filmed: the giant ape beating his chest atop the Empire State Building and swiping at the biplanes that have come to take him down. King Kong's tragic end at the top of the tower holds up remarkably well almost eight decades later.

Sleepless in Seattle - 1993
Most of this film, starring Tom Hanks and Meg Ryan, takes place in Seattle. However, a crucial part of the plot was shot in New York City.

Paris Is Burning - 1990
Do you know about the origins of vogue dance? Are you familiar with the legends Benny Ninja or the House of Xtravaganza. If not, Jennie Livingston's documentary about the New York City gay ballroom scene is essential viewing.

Superman - 1978
The original Superman movie, complete with Christopher Reeve soaring past Battery Park and the old skyline. New York is on display all over this classic. If you've never seen it, treat yourself.
